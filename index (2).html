Control Testing Results

Privileged Access Reviews
Failed
User Access Management · COBIT DSS05.04
Quarterly privileged access reviews are not being conducted. Sampling of 30 accounts revealed 9 users retaining access beyond role termination. No evidence of formal access recertification process.
Risk Level:High
Password Complexity & Expiry Policy
Failed
Password Policies · ISO 27001 A.9.4.3
Password policy enforces minimum 8 characters but lacks complexity requirements. Passwords expire every 180 days — exceeds the 90-day threshold. Multi-factor authentication not enforced on core banking systems.
Risk Level:High
Change Management & CAB Process
Partial
Change Management · COBIT BAI06.01
Change Advisory Board (CAB) exists but emergency changes (17% of total) bypass standard approval. Rollback plans documented for only 62% of changes tested. Post-implementation reviews are inconsistent.
Risk Level:Medium
Backup & Recovery Procedures
Passed
Backup & Recovery · ISO 27001 A.12.3.1
Daily backups confirmed for all Tier-1 systems with offsite replication. Last full recovery test completed 47 days ago — within policy. RTO of 4 hours and RPO of 1 hour met in simulation.
Risk Level:Low
Incident Response & Escalation
Failed
Incident Management · COBIT DSS02.02
Incident response plan not updated since 2021. Tabletop exercise not conducted in current year. 3 of 5 critical incidents reviewed exceeded SLA resolution times with no documented escalation evidence.
Risk Level:High
Security Event Logging & SIEM
Partial
Logging & Monitoring · ISO 27001 A.12.4
SIEM platform deployed and collecting logs from 78% of in-scope systems. Legacy core banking module excluded due to compatibility. Log retention is 90 days — below the 1-year requirement for financial institutions.
Risk Level:Medium
Physical Data Centre Access
Passed
Physical Security · ISO 27001 A.11.1
Data centre access controlled via biometric + badge. Visitor logs maintained and reviewed monthly. CCTV coverage confirmed. Last physical access review completed within quarter. Clean desk policy enforced.
Risk Level:Low
Vendor Due Diligence & SLA Monitoring
Partial
Vendor Management · COBIT APO10.03
Vendor register maintained but only 60% of critical vendors have current SOC 2 Type II reports on file. SLA performance reviews conducted semi-annually rather than quarterly. No sub-processor visibility documented.
Risk Level:Medium

Executive Summary

Key Observations

  • Access management controls present the highest residual risk — orphaned accounts and absent MFA on core systems expose the bank to insider threat and credential-based attacks
  • Log retention below regulatory minimums creates legal and forensic risk, particularly in the event of a breach investigation
  • Incident response capability is not audit-ready — the 2021 plan and lack of exercises present a significant operational resilience gap
  • Physical security and backup controls are operating effectively; these represent the strongest areas of the control environment
  • Vendor management requires uplift — the bank has outsourcing risk without adequate third-party assurance documentation

Management Action Plan

  • Immediate (0–30 days): Disable 9 identified orphaned accounts; enforce MFA on all privileged access paths
  • Short-term (30–90 days): Update incident response plan; extend SIEM log retention to 12 months; add rollback plan to CAB template
  • Medium-term (90–180 days): Integrate legacy banking module into SIEM; complete tabletop exercise; collect SOC 2 reports from all critical vendors
  • Ongoing: Establish quarterly access certification cycle; implement vendor performance scorecard; schedule annual IRP reviews
  • Ownership: CISO accountable for access and logging items; IT Operations for change management; Procurement for vendor program
