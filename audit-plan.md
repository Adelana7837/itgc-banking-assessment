# ITGC Audit Plan — Banking Environment
**Project:** IT General Controls Assessment  
**Environment:** Simulated Banking Institution  
**Audit Type:** IT General Controls (ITGC)  
**Status:** Approved  

---

## 1. Audit Objectives

To evaluate the design and operating effectiveness of IT General Controls supporting the bank's core financial systems, in accordance with:
- COBIT 2019 Governance Framework
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Information Security Standard
- CISA IT Audit Methodology

**Specific Objectives:**
1. Determine whether IT controls adequately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of financial data
2. Identify control deficiencies and assess their potential impact on financial reporting
3. Provide management with actionable recommendations to remediate identified gaps

---

## 2. Scope

### In-Scope Systems
| System | Classification | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Core Banking Platform (CBS) | Tier 1 – Critical | IT Operations |
| Internet Banking Portal | Tier 1 – Critical | Digital Banking |
| Treasury Management System | Tier 1 – Critical | Finance |
| Data Centre Infrastructure | Infrastructure | IT Infrastructure |
| Active Directory / IAM Platform | Identity | IT Security |
| SIEM / Log Management | Security | IT Security |

### In-Scope Control Domains
1. User Access Management
2. Password Policies
3. Change Management
4. Backup & Recovery
5. Incident Management
6. Logging & Monitoring
7. Physical Security
8. Vendor Management

### Out of Scope
- Application-level controls within individual banking products
- Financial statement auditing
- Network penetration testing

---

## 3. Methodology

### Phase 1 — Planning (Weeks 1–2)
- Kick-off meeting with IT management and CISO
- Obtain and review IT policies, procedures, and prior audit reports
- Risk assessment to prioritise control domains
- Develop audit programme and testing procedures

### Phase 2 — Fieldwork (Weeks 3–6)
- Conduct walkthroughs with process owners
- Perform attribute sampling for each control area (n=25–30 per population)
- Request and review evidence (screenshots, logs, reports, approvals)
- Document test results in the Control Testing Workbook

### Phase 3 — Reporting (Weeks 7–8)
- Validate findings with management
- Rate findings by risk level (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
- Issue draft report for management response
- Finalise report and present to Audit Committee

## 4. Sampling Approach

| Control Area | Population | Sample Size | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| User Access Reviews | 450 user accounts | 30 | Random |
| Password Settings | All systems (12) | 12 | Census |
| Change Requests | 280 changes (period) | 30 | Random |
| Backup Completions | 90 days of logs | 25 | Random |
| Incident Tickets | 180 incidents | 25 | Judgmental |
| Vendor Contracts | 48 critical vendors | 20 | Risk-based |

## 7. Risk Rating Definitions

| Rating | Likelihood × Impact | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | 20–25 | Immediate risk of material loss, breach, or regulatory sanction |
| **High** | 13–19 | Significant control failure requiring prompt remediation |
| **Medium** | 6–12 | Moderate weakness; compensating controls may exist |
| **Low** | 1–5 | Minor gap with limited exposure; low-priority remediation |
